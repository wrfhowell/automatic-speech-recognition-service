# Backend Transcription Take-Home

You're tasked with building an audio transcription service that transcribes audio files of patient/provider conversations and returns transcripts. Clients will submit jobs containing chunks of audio they would like transcribed and stitched into a single transcript. For now, these audio chunks will just be one of a few hardcoded URI strings. You will not implement the ASR (Automatic Speech Recognition) model directly — we'll provide mocked APIs which simulate making an API call to an ASR model hosted on another machine.

## What we're evaluating

Build this **as if you were shipping it to production at Ambience**, not as a proof of concept. The closer your submitted code is to a real production system, the stronger the signal. We'll evaluate both your system design and your implementation.

## ASR characteristics

- The ASR model service can only process audio chunks ≤ 5 minutes in length
    - You can assume the chunks clients send you will be ≤ 5 mins
- Each call to the ASR model has non-trivial latency 5-10s, but we’d still like to be able to return the full transcript to the client within 20 seconds in the happy case.
- The ASR model service has some flakiness
    - anticipate 1/20 of requests to fail transiently
    - some audio segments will always fail
- The ASR model enforces a concurrency limit of 100 simultaneous requests (although this number will increase in the future). Going over this value may result in a ban, or financial implications from this third-party vendor.

## Your Service requirements (functional)

Your service should support the following interface:

```
POST /transcribe
  input:
	  audioChunkPaths=string[]
	  userId=string
  returns:
		jobId=string

Type: TranscriptResult {
    transcriptText=string
    chunkStatuses={ audioPath: chunkStatus }
    jobStatus=string,
		completedTime=DateTime/ISOString/etc
}

GET /transcript/{jobId}
  returns: TranscriptResult

GET /transcript/search
	Accepts queryParams:
		jobStatus=string
    userId=string
  returns:
		TranscriptResult[]
```

## Your Service requirements (Non functional)

1. **Fault tolerance** — jobs resume gracefully after a server/worker restart (implies a persistence layer for in-flight job state).
2. **ASR concurrency limit adherence** — your service must not exceed 100 simultaneous ASR requests. Going over this limit may result in vendor bans or financial penalties.
3. **Latency** — end-to-end transcript latency should target <20s in the happy path. Parallelize ASR calls within the concurrency budget.
4. **Scalability** - the system is able to scale without requiring a complete redesign

## Scale

- **You need to support 1,000 daily active users today.**
- **We expect traffic to grow ~10x within 6 months** as we sign new enterprise contracts. Your system should be scalable so we can grow into that.
- **In your presentation and/or system diagram, walk us through how your design handles this growth** — which levers you'd pull, what scales as-is, what needs to change, and the tradeoffs you considered.

Workload datapoints to anchor sizing:

- 1000 Daily active users, 8 hour shifts, 30 minute visits (expect Daily active users to scale to 10k in 6 months)
- Visits are usually 30 minutes, but sometimes can spike to 120 minutes
- Human speech generally contains ~140 words per minute
- Audio files are ~ 1MB per minute
